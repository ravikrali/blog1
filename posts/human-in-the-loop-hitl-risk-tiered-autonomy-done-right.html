Human-in-the-Loop (HITL): Risk-Tiered Autonomy Done Right

A practical tier model that balances velocity with accountability—especially in regulated environments.

The HITL tiers

A mature HITL model defines explicit autonomy tiers: Fully Autonomous, Notification Only, Approval Required, Human Only. The key is mapping tiers to risk categories and continuously revisiting the mapping as confidence grows.

Escalation criteria

Escalate when changes touch security boundaries, customer-facing systems, production data, or regulatory evidence. Also escalate for novel scenarios outside training coverage or when confidence drops.

Designing review UX

Make approvals fast: show diffs, policy checks, test results, and a concise rationale. Humans should supervise decisions, not hunt for context.

Implementation notes

Use this as a reference pattern, then tailor it to your enterprise boundaries, risk tiers, and standards library. The goal is repeatable execution with measurable governance.
