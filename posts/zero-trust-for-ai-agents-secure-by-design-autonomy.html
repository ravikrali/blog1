Zero Trust for AI Agents: Secure-by-Design Autonomy

Treat agents as untrusted actors by default: least privilege, JIT access, continuous verification, and comprehensive audit trails.

On this page

Core principles Practical controls Auditable operations
flowchart TD ID[Per-Agent Identity] --> RBAC[Least-Privilege RBAC] RBAC --> JIT[Just-in-Time Tokens] JIT --> NET[Network Isolation] NET --> ACT[Agent Actions] ACT --> LOG[Immutable Audit Logs] ACT --> POL[Policy Gate] POL -->|Sensitive| HUM[Human Approval]

Core principles

Least privilege, just-in-time access, continuous verification, comprehensive auditing, and assume breach. These principles reduce blast radius when agents behave unexpectedly.

Practical controls

Isolated execution environments, RBAC per agent function, short-lived tokens, private networking, data masking for PII, and mandatory scanning of generated code.

Auditable operations

Every action needs a correlation ID, policy context, and human approval record when applicable. Security isn’t a checklist—it’s an operational system.

Implementation notes

Use this as a reference pattern, then tailor it to your enterprise boundaries, risk tiers, and standards library. The goal is repeatable execution with measurable governance.
